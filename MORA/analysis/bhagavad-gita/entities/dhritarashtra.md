## Executive Summary

- **Character Analysis:** Dhritarashtra represents the blindness of attachment ("Moha"). Physically blind, he is also spiritually blind to the righteousness of the Pandavas due to his excessive love for his son Duryodhana. He is the passive enabler of the conflict. While he does not fight personally, his failure to exercise his authority as King to prevent the injustice makes him morally culpable for the entire war. He constantly seeks reassurance from Sanjaya but fails to act on the wisdom provided.
