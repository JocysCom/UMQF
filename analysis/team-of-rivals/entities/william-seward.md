## Background
- Former Governor of New York and US Senator. A leading anti-slavery Whig who became a Republican. Lost the 1860 nomination to Lincoln but became Secretary of State.

## Motivation
- **Drives:** To serve the country; to be at the center of power; to see the triumph of free labor and the expansion of the American empire.
- **Philosophy:** Believes in a "higher law" than the Constitution but is pragmatic in application; an optimist who believes in the inevitable progress of civilization.
- **Future Behavior Prediction:** Will continue to serve loyally; will seek to expand American territory (Alaska).

## Relationships
- **Abraham Lincoln:** A deep, affectionate friendship; they are "two spirits" who understand each other.
- **Thurlow Weed:** His political alter ego and lifelong friend.
- **Frances Seward:** His wife and conscience; she pushes him toward radicalism while he navigates pragmatism.
- **Salmon Chase:** A bitter rivalry; Seward views Chase as rigid and ambitious.

## Resource and Environmental Adaptation
- **Survival Skills:** Social adaptability; resilience in the face of defeat and physical injury (assassination attempt).
- **Tech Use:** Uses the telegraph and the press masterfully.

## Long-Term Impact and Legacy Forecast
